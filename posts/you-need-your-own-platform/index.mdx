title: "You need your own platform"
date: "2023-01-30"
description: "Discover the benefits of building a personal website and blogging as a software engineer."
---

I built my personal website to showcase my personal brand and share my experiences, projects, and thoughts through a custom blog platform. I use it as a training ground for me to learn new technology. The process of building and rebuilding it from scratch has been an amazing journey filled with new discoveries and growth.

Blogging regularly on my site has been instrumental in getting noticed by potential employers and landing my current job. It allows me to demonstrate my skills and passion for the field.

It became a topic of discussion during job interviews, and I've had the valuable opportunity to explain my thought process in building the site.

I learned from [Jessica Deen](https://twitter.com/jldeen)'s talk to us at [Vets Who Code](https://vetswhocode.io/) that the key to demonstrating your skills is identifying in-demand technical skills and applying them through practice and real-world projects, like building a personal website.

This strategy has proven successful for Jessica. I applied her lesson and it worked for me too. And there's no reason it can't work for you too. Nothing is stopping you.

Also realize that writing is a crucial skill in software engineering, and writing well can greatly advance your career. That's why I encourage everyone to start a blog and document their journey. And the best way to improve your writing is to write more, so why not start today?

Having your own platform is crucial in today's digital world. It not only helps you build your personal brand but also opens doors to new opportunities.

So, why not start your own blog today and see where it takes you? Check out my [updated project on GitHub](https://github.com/jpreagan/jpreagan.com) and feel free to use it as a starting point for your own.
